Coding Projects

Projects that give bootcamp graduates the opportunity to roll up their sleeves and test their skills.

Once you’ve finished your coding bootcamp, you’re advised to ‘keep coding’. This makes sense as it keeps your skills sharp and will hopefully allow you to expand your project portfolio for that all-important job application.
But what should you code? Websites like CodeWars offer hundreds of coding ‘katas’ for you to work through. But they tend to be small algorithm exercises, rather than roll-your-sleeves-up web projects.
If you could add a few substantial web apps to your portfolio, it would go a long way to convincing a potential employer that your basic skills are strong and that you know how to ‘fly solo’.
